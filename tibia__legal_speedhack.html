Legal Speedhack

back

This trick is common knowledge among gamers not only in MMOs but pretty much everywhere.

Having problems moving fast just after changing directions?
That's because Windows, by default, has a relatively high keystroke repeat delay, so as to make typing less error-prone. The repeat delay is the timespan between you pressing a key and Windows recognising that you're willingly holding it down to keep walking into a certain direction. If, when changing directions you only move one sqm, then stand still for a split second, this is the reason. Then there's the repeat rate, not overly important to lower levels, but significant from level 80+ and strong haste.
The repeat rate is the delay -whilst holding down a key- between two repetitions. If, despite perfect FPS ping, you feel like you were standing still for a fraction of a second between every step, you should consider changing the repeat rate as well.

To change it, go to:
Control Panel > Keyboard > Speed > and move both sliders to the right (short/fast respectively)



This may feel awkward at first, but you'll get used to it.
Plus, it's waaaaaaaaaaaaaay better to spam people.
(with default settings it would have taken 3x the time to type those 'a's by holding down rather than spamming the key)
