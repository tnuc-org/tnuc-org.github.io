How to get around the MSN/Live Messenger 2011 Update

back

The 2011 version of MSN/Windows Live Messenger is a horrible, horrible piece of software. By moving more and more simple features such as changing your name (you can't go without a last name anymore) to their Live website they are trying to forcefully establish Windows Live as a social platform.
But it's shit.

Until June 20th 2012 getting around the 2011 update was as simple as re-installing with the offline installer of an early v14.x (2009) version, but now that the update is mandatory for all Vista/Windows 7 users you'll have to make it believe it's running on Windows XP still, since the 2011 version only supports Vista and up.

If you haven't yet updated, jump to step 5:
1) Go to the "Control Panel", then "Programs and Features"
2) Scroll down to "Windows Live Essentials", right-click and fully remove it.
3) The uninstaller will force you to reboot your computer.
4) Install the 2009 Version of Live Messenger (mirror: oldapps.com)
5) Close MSN (it will autostart after install)
6) Right-click the desktop/startmenu/wherever shortcut and go to "Properties"
7) On the "Compatibility" Tab, set it to "Windows XP SP3" Compatibility, apply, close
8) Start MSN, no more prompting you to update
